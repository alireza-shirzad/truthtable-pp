\section{PIOPs for Wild-Card Pattern Matching}

In this section we develop PIOPs for SQL \emph{wild-card} pattern matching --- the predicates written with the \texttt{LIKE} operator. A \texttt{LIKE} pattern may use two kinds of wild-card: the multi-character \texttt{\%}, which matches any (possibly empty) run of characters, and the single-character \texttt{\_}, which matches exactly one character. \Cref{fig:wildcard-taxonomy} shows the taxonomy of patterns we support: three kinds --- single-character, multi-character, and mixed --- where the single- and multi-character kinds each come in a single-factor and a multi-factor mode.

\subsection{Multi-Character}
\label{sec:multi-char-pattern-matching}

The most common kind of string pattern matching in natural SQL workloads is a multi-character pattern matching which is of the form $s_1 \% s_2 \% \dots \% s_k$, where each $s_i$ is a literal string (a factor) and \% matches an arbitrary-length run of characters — so the predicate holds iff the factors $s_1 \% s_2 \% \dots \% s_k$ occur in order within the value, with the leading and trailing \% (or their absence) determining whether the match is anchored to the start and/or end of the string.

Depending on the number of factors, we call the expressions as a single-factor or multi-factor pattern. We start by describing protocols for single-factor multi-character pattern matching in \cref{sec:single-factor-multi-char-pattern-matching}, then lift the construction to a multi-factor setting in \cref{sec:multi-factor-multi-char-pattern-matching}.

\subsubsection{Single-Factor}

A single-factor multi-character pattern matches a single literal factor against the value, and---depending on where the \% wild-cards are placed---comes in three forms:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \emph{prefix} (\texttt{'Promo\%'}): the factor must appear at the start of the value;
  \item \emph{suffix} (\texttt{'\%BRASS'}): the factor must appear at the end of the value;
  \item \emph{infix} (\texttt{'\%green\%'}): the factor may appear anywhere in the value.
\end{itemize}

We present a single general protocol (\cref{piop:single-factor-multi-char-pattern-matching}) that proves all three of prefix, suffix and infix matching against a length-$k$ pattern $\mathsf{str}$. We first give the intuition for the infix case, which is the hardest, and then explain why prefix and suffix fall out as strictly easier special cases.

\parhead{Proving infix} The verifier receives a new pair of activators $\mathsf{a_h^{new}},\mathsf{a_c^{new}}$ and must check that, relative to the old activators $\mathsf{a_h^{old}},\mathsf{a_c^{old}}$, they keep exactly the words containing $\mathsf{str}$ and switch off all the rest. The protocol does this in four steps. \begin{enumerate*}[label=(\roman*)]
  \item \emph{Validity.} The verifier first checks that the new activators are well-formed: boolean, mutually consistent (a word is on iff all of its characters are on), and contained in the old activators (no inactive word is revived).
  \item \emph{Localization.} A match of $\mathsf{str}$ can begin at any of the $\mathsf{l}-k+1$ positions of a word, so naively the verifier would have to inspect a whole window of $k$ characters at every position. To collapse each window to a single slot, the verifier draws random coefficients $r_0,\dots,r_{k-1}$ and the parties form the \emph{window fingerprint} $\mathsf{wf} := \sum_{\delta} r_\delta\,\mathsf{c}^{(\delta)}$ (a random linear combination of the rotations of the character column) and the \emph{pattern fingerprint} $\mathsf{pf} := \sum_{\delta} r_\delta\,\mathsf{str}[\delta]$. Their difference $\mathsf{diff} := \mathsf{wf}-\mathsf{pf}$ then has $\mathsf{diff}[p]=0$ exactly when the length-$k$ window starting at $p$ equals $\mathsf{str}$ (up to a negligible fingerprint collision). The whole question now reduces to where $\mathsf{diff}$ vanishes.
  \item \emph{No false negatives.} Every word that is switched off must really fail to contain $\mathsf{str}$. The verifier asks that $\mathsf{diff}$ have \emph{no} zero among the candidate slots of those words --- a single no-zero check --- which certifies that the pattern occurs nowhere inside them.
  \item \emph{No false positives.} Every word that is kept on must really contain $\mathsf{str}$, i.e. $\mathsf{diff}$ must have \emph{at least one} zero among its candidate slots. The difficulty is that this zero can sit anywhere in the word, so the prover points it out: it sends a selector that is $1$ on one matching slot of each kept word and $0$ elsewhere, and the verifier checks that $\mathsf{diff}$ indeed vanishes there. A short series of checks (booleanity, no duplicates, and a counting argument) then confirms the selector is honest --- it marks one genuine slot per kept word and nothing else.
\end{enumerate*}

\parhead{Prefix and suffix} These are the infix protocol with one simplification: the matching slot is no longer ambiguous. A prefix match can only begin at a word's first character and a suffix match only at its last, so the verifier already knows where $\mathsf{diff}$ must vanish. The prover therefore sends no selector and skips all of the checks that would establish its validity.

\label{sec:single-factor-multi-char-pattern-matching}
\begin{DefinePIOP}{Single-Factor Multi-Character (SFMC) Pattern Matching}{single-factor-multi-char-pattern-matching}[SFMC Pattern Matching]
  \begin{enumerate}[nosep]
    \item (\textit{Activator validity check})
    \begin{enumerate}[nosep]
      \item $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:boolean-check} on $\mathsf{a_c^{new}}$ and on $\mathsf{a_h^{new}}$.
      \item $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:act-cons} on $(\mathsf{a_c^{new}}, \mathsf{a_h^{new}})$.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item (\textit{Filtering by length}) $\PIOPProver$ computes $\mathsf{a_h^{old'}}$ and $\mathsf{a_c^{old'}}$ which is the result of filtering the strings with length less than $k$, then $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:len-filter} $(\mathsf{a_h^{old}}, \mathsf{a_c^{old}}, \mathsf{a_h^{old'}}, \mathsf{a_c^{old'}})$ with threshold $k$.
    \item (\textit{Containment}) $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:zerocheck} on $\mathsf{a_h^{new}}\cdot(1-\mathsf{a_h^{old'}})$.
    \item (\textit{Rotated character columns}) $\PIOPProver$ sends $\mathsf{c}^{(1)},\ldots,\mathsf{c}^{(k-1)}$, each $\mathsf{c}^{(\delta)}$ a rotation of $\mathsf{c}^{(\delta-1)}$ by $-1$ (so $\mathsf{c}^{(\delta)}[p] = \mathsf{c}[p+\delta]$) and $\mathsf{c}^{(0)} := \mathsf{c}$. Then the parties invoke \cref{piop:rotcheck} on each $(\mathsf{c}^{(\delta-1)}, \mathsf{c}^{(\delta)}, -1)$.
    \item (\textit{Window fingerprinting}) $\PIOPVerifier$ samples $r_0,\ldots,r_{k-1} \samples \F$ and sends them to $\PIOPProver$. 
    \begin{enumerate}[nosep]
      \item $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ form the windowed fingerprint of the character polynomial as $\mathsf{wf} := \sum_{\delta=0}^{k-1} r_\delta\,\mathsf{c}^{(\delta)}$.
      \item $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ form the fingerprint of the pattern as $\mathsf{pf} := \sum_{\delta=0}^{k-1} r_\delta\,\mathsf{str}[\delta]$
      \item $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ form the difference polynomial as $\mathsf{diff} := \mathsf{wf}-\mathsf{pf}$
    \end{enumerate} 
    \item (\textit{Attention mask}) $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ fix the attention mask $\mathsf{att\_mask}$ as follows:
    \begin{enumerate}[nosep]
      \item if the pattern is \emph{prefix}, they set $\mathsf{att\_mask} := \mathsf{a_c^{old'}}\cdot\mathsf{s_b}$
      \item else if the pattern is \emph{suffix}, $\PIOPProver$ computes $\mathsf{att\_mask} := \rho_{-k}(\mathsf{a_c^{old'}}\cdot\mathsf{s_b})$ and sends it to the verifier. Then the parties invoke \cref{piop:rotcheck} on $(\mathsf{a_c^{old'}}\cdot\mathsf{s_b},\,\mathsf{att\_mask},\,-k)$.
      \item else (where the pattern is \emph{infix}), $\PIOPProver$ sends the rotated boundary selectors $\mathsf{s_b}^{(1)},\ldots,\mathsf{s_b}^{(k-1)}$ ($\mathsf{s_b}^{(0)}:=\mathsf{s_b}$, each a rotation of the previous by $-1$). Then the parties invoke \cref{piop:rotcheck} for each $(\mathsf{s_b}^{(j-1)},\mathsf{s_b}^{(j)},-1)$, and set $\mathsf{att\_mask} := \mathsf{a_c^{old'}}\cdot\bigl(1-\textstyle\sum_{j=1}^{k-1}\mathsf{s_b}^{(j)}\bigr)$.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item (\textit{False negative check}) $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:nozero-check} on the column with data $\mathsf{diff}$ and activator $\mathsf{att\_mask}\cdot(1-\mathsf{a_c^{new}})$.
    \item (\textit{False positive check})
    \begin{enumerate}[nosep]
      \item If the pattern is \emph{prefix} or \emph{suffix}, $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:zerocheck} on the column with data $\mathsf{diff}$ and activator $\mathsf{att\_mask}\cdot\mathsf{a_c^{new}}$.
      \item If the pattern is \emph{infix}, $\PIOPProver$ sends a false-positive marker $\mathsf{m_{fp}}$, then
      \begin{enumerate}[nosep]
        \item $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:boolean-check} on $\mathsf{m_{fp}}$;
        \item $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:zerocheck} on $\mathsf{m_{fp}}\cdot(1-\mathsf{a_c^{new}})$ and on $\mathsf{m_{fp}}\cdot(1-\mathsf{att\_mask})$.
        \item $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:zerocheck} on the column with data $\mathsf{diff}$ and activator $\mathsf{m_{fp}}$;
        \item $\PIOPProver$ sends $n_{\mathsf{m}}$ to $\PIOPVerifier$, then $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke invoke \cref{piop:sumcheck} twice, for $\sum_{\vec{x}}\mathsf{m_{fp}}(\vec{x})=n_{\mathsf{m}}$ and $\sum_{\vec{x}}\mathsf{a_h^{new}}(\vec{x})=n_{\mathsf{m}}$.
        \item $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:nodup-check} on the column with data $\mathsf{src}$ and activator $\mathsf{m_{fp}}$.
      \end{enumerate}
    \end{enumerate}
  \end{enumerate}
\end{DefinePIOP}

\begin{proof}
We argue completeness and soundness, assuming each invoked sub-PIOP is complete and sound for its relation. Call a string \emph{eligible} if it is active under $\mathsf{a_h^{old}}$ and has length at least $k$; by the soundness of \cref{piop:len-filter} these are exactly the strings marked by $\mathsf{a_h^{old'}}$, and we write $E := \{\,i : \mathsf{a_h^{old'}}[i]=1\,\}$. Let $\mathsf{att\_mask}$ be the attention mask of the chosen filter; a string \emph{matches} if one of its candidate windows (the positions $p$ in it with $\mathsf{att\_mask}[p]=1$) equals $\mathsf{str}$. The filter must keep exactly the eligible matching strings, and we write $M := \{\,i : \mathsf{a_h^{new}}[i]=1\,\}$ for the kept set.

\parhead{Completeness}
For an honest prover the rotation checks pass, so $\mathsf{c}^{(\delta)}[p]=\mathsf{c}[p+\delta]$, $\mathsf{diff}[p]=0$ exactly at the occurrences of $\mathsf{str}$, and $\mathsf{att\_mask}$ marks the candidate windows of each eligible string. The honest new activators keep exactly the eligible matching strings, so they are boolean, consistent, and contained in $\mathsf{a_h^{old'}}$, passing the activator-validity and containment checks. Every dropped eligible string is non-matching, so all of its candidate windows have $\mathsf{diff}\neq0$ and the false-negative no-zero check passes. For an anchored filter the single candidate of each kept string equals $\mathsf{str}$, so the false-positive zerocheck passes; for the infix filter the prover marks with $\mathsf{m_{fp}}$ one candidate window equal to $\mathsf{str}$ in each kept string --- boolean, satisfying $\mathsf{m_{fp}}\leq\mathsf{a_c^{new}}$ and $\mathsf{m_{fp}}\leq\mathsf{att\_mask}$, with $\mathsf{diff}=0$ on its support, one mark per kept string, matching the announced count $n_{\mathsf{m}}=|M|$ --- so the false-positive checks pass. Hence the honest transcript is accepted.

\parhead{Soundness}
Suppose $\PIOPVerifier$ accepts. By the soundness of \cref{piop:len-filter} (\cref{sec:length-filtering}), $E=\{\,i:\mathsf{a_h^{old'}}[i]=1\,\}$ is exactly the set of eligible strings and $\mathsf{a_c^{old'}}$ is on exactly at their characters.

The rotation checks force $\mathsf{c}^{(\delta)}[p]=\mathsf{c}[p+\delta]$ for every $\delta$, so $\mathsf{wf}[p]=\sum_\delta r_\delta\,\mathsf{c}[p+\delta]$ is the fingerprint of the length-$k$ window of $\mathsf{c}$ at $p$ and $\mathsf{diff}[p]=\mathsf{wf}[p]-\mathsf{pf}$. A window equal to $\mathsf{str}$ gives $\mathsf{diff}[p]=0$ identically; a window differing from $\mathsf{str}$ gives $\mathsf{diff}[p]=0$ only when the verifier's $r_0,\ldots,r_{k-1}$ are a root of a fixed nonzero degree-$1$ polynomial, so by Schwartz--Zippel and a union bound over the $N$ positions, except with probability $N(k-1)/|\F|$,
\[ \mathsf{diff}[p]=0 \iff \text{the length-$k$ window of $\mathsf{c}$ at $p$ equals }\mathsf{str},\qquad\text{for every }p. \]
We condition on this event; note the direction $\mathsf{diff}[p]\neq0\Rightarrow$ window $\neq\mathsf{str}$ holds \emph{unconditionally}. The rotation checks on the boundary selectors make $\mathsf{att\_mask}$ the attention mask of the chosen filter: within each eligible string it marks the single anchored window (prefix, suffix) or all $\mathsf{l}-k+1$ valid starts (infix), with $\mathsf{att\_mask}\leq\mathsf{a_c^{old'}}$, and in every case $\mathsf{att\_mask}[p]=1$ guarantees that the window at $p$ lies entirely within the string $\mathsf{src}[p]$.

The activator-validity check makes $\mathsf{a_h^{new}},\mathsf{a_c^{new}}$ boolean and forces $\mathsf{a_c^{new}}[j]=\mathsf{a_h^{new}}[\mathsf{src}[j]]$ for every character $j$, so $\mathsf{a_c^{new}}$ is constant on each string and equals its header bit. The containment zerocheck $\mathsf{a_h^{new}}(1-\mathsf{a_h^{old'}})=0$ forces $M\subseteq E$.

\emph{No false positives.} For an anchored filter, $\mathsf{att\_mask}$ marks one candidate per eligible string, and the false-positive zerocheck forces $\mathsf{diff}=0$ wherever $\mathsf{att\_mask}\cdot\mathsf{a_c^{new}}=1$, i.e.\ at the candidate of each kept string; since that window lies within its string and $\mathsf{diff}=0$ means it equals $\mathsf{str}$, every kept string matches. For the infix filter, the two confinement zerochecks give $\mathsf{m_{fp}}\leq\mathsf{a_c^{new}}$ and $\mathsf{m_{fp}}\leq\mathsf{att\_mask}$, and the occurrence zerocheck gives $\mathsf{diff}=0$ on the support of $\mathsf{m_{fp}}$; thus every mark $p$ has $\mathsf{a_c^{new}}[p]=1$ (so $\mathsf{src}[p]\in M$), $\mathsf{att\_mask}[p]=1$ (a candidate window lying inside $\mathsf{src}[p]$), and $\mathsf{diff}[p]=0$ (that window equals $\mathsf{str}$), so $\mathsf{src}[p]$ matches. The no-duplicate check on $(\mathsf{src},\mathsf{m_{fp}})$ puts the marks in distinct strings, and the two sum checks give $\sum\mathsf{m_{fp}}=n_{\mathsf{m}}=\sum\mathsf{a_h^{new}}=|M|$, so the $|M|$ marks lie in distinct strings of $M$ and hit every string of $M$ exactly once. Either way, every kept string is an eligible match.

\emph{No false negatives.} Let $i\in E\setminus M$ be a dropped eligible string. By consistency every character of $i$ has $\mathsf{a_c^{new}}=0$, so $\mathsf{att\_mask}\cdot(1-\mathsf{a_c^{new}})=\mathsf{att\_mask}$ on string $i$, which is $1$ at each of its candidate windows. The false-negative no-zero check therefore forces $\mathsf{diff}[p]\neq0$ there, so by the unconditional direction none of $i$'s candidate windows equals $\mathsf{str}$. Hence $i$ does not match. Contrapositively, every eligible matching string lies in $M$.

Combining the two directions, $M$ is exactly the set of eligible matching strings --- the strings passing the chosen prefix, suffix, or infix filter --- except with the fingerprint-collision probability $N(k-1)/|\F|$ and the soundness errors of the invoked sub-PIOPs.
\end{proof}



\subsubsection{Multi-Factor}

A multi-factor multi-character pattern matches several literal factors against each value, with a wild-card \% between consecutive factors absorbing an arbitrary run of characters. Writing the pattern as $p_1 \% p_2 \% \dots \% p_t$, a value satisfies it iff the factors $\mathcal{P}=\{p_1,\dots,p_t\}$ all occur within it \emph{in this order}.

The objective of the protocol is to establish that the updated activators $\mathsf{a_h^{new}}$ and $\mathsf{a_c^{new}}$ correctly capture the result of the pattern matching. As in \cref{piop:single-factor-multi-char-pattern-matching}, the first steps check the validity of the provided activators (\cref{mfmc:validity}), filter by length (\cref{mfmc:filter}), and enforce containment (\cref{mfmc:containment}).

The prover and the verifier then run \cref{piop:single-factor-multi-char-pattern-matching} for each factor $p\in\mathcal{P}$, obtaining a separate activator $\mathsf{a^{new}_{h,p}}$ per factor. It is important to note that a string satisfying every factor predicate \emph{separately} need not satisfy the full pattern $\mathcal{P}$: in general $\mathsf{a^{new}_{h}}\neq\mathsf{a^{\cap}_h}$, where $\mathsf{a^{\cap}_h}:=\prod_{p\in \mathcal{P}}\mathsf{a^{new}_{h,p}}$. The reason is that the order of the factors matters. For the pattern \texttt{\%ab\%cd\%}, for instance, the value \texttt{xabycdz} satisfies the predicate while \texttt{xcdyabz} does not---even though both contain \texttt{ab} and \texttt{cd}---because there \texttt{cd} precedes \texttt{ab}. Hence the finally-active words form a subset of those active in every factor, which is enforced in \cref{mfmc:cap-containment}. The rest of the protocol establishes that the words deactivated between $\mathsf{a^{\cap}_h}$ and $\mathsf{a^{new}_{h}}$ fail to admit the correct ordering (there are no false negatives), while the words that survive into $\mathsf{a^{new}_{h}}$ do satisfy the ordering constraints (there are no false positives).

\parhead{Ordering False-Positive Check} The easier direction is to verify that the words surviving into the final activator---those active in both $\mathsf{a_h^\cap}$ and $\mathsf{a_h^{new}}$---do respect the required ordering. For this the verifier needs a handle on where each factor sits inside its word: at \cref{mfmc:false-pos-word-level-occ-ind} the prover sends, for every factor $p_i\in\mathcal{P}$, the word-level offset column $\mathsf{offset}_{p_i}$ recording the position at which $p_i$ is placed. Then, at \cref{mfmc:false-pos-verdict}, for each consecutive pair $(p_{i},p_{i+1})$ the verifier checks that $\mathsf{offset}_{p_{i+1}}$ exceeds $\mathsf{offset}_{p_{i}}$ by at least $|p_i|$, so the factors appear in order and without overlap. The remaining checks ensure that each $\mathsf{offset}_{p_i}$ is itself well-formed---that it genuinely marks an occurrence of $p$ inside the word.

\parhead{Ordering False-Negative Check} The trickier direction is to establish that every discarded string---one that contains all the factors (active in $\mathsf{a_h^\cap}$) but is switched off in the output (inactive in $\mathsf{a_h^{new}}$)---genuinely fails to admit the factors in the required order. The difficulty is that a cheating prover has many ways to discard a perfectly valid ordering simply by reporting the factor placements incorrectly. Consider the pattern \texttt{\%ab\%bd\%bd\%} ($\mathcal{P}=\{p_1,p_2,p_3\}=\{\texttt{ab},\texttt{bd},\texttt{bd}\}$), in which the factor \texttt{bd} repeats, together with the string \texttt{xabybdzbd}: it matches the pattern (\texttt{ab} at $1$, \texttt{bd} at $4$, \texttt{bd} at $7$) and should be active in the output. A cheating prover, however, could report the placements of $p_2$ and $p_3$ as $7$ and $4$---swapping the two \texttt{bd} occurrences---so that the offsets appear out of order and the string is wrongly discarded.

To rule out this attack, the verifier forces $\mathsf{offset}_{p_i}$ to be formed greedily: each factor is placed at the earliest match at or past the end of the previous factor, never skipping a valid occurrence. This greedy placement is optimal---if any in-order embedding exists, the greedy one does too---so once the verifier is convinced that even the greedy offsets cannot place all factors in order, no placement can, and the string is rightly discarded.


To enforce greedy offsetting, the core idea is to keep track of three things: \begin{enumerate*}[label=(\roman*)]
  \item \emph{all} occurrences of the factors, which is denoted by $\mathsf{occurs_p}$ masking polynomial.
  \item The set of allowed offsets that a factor can get, which starts when the previous factor is completely finished, i.e. $\mathsf{offset}{p_{j-1}}+|p_{j-1}|$, which is denoted by $\mathsf{allowed\_offset}$ masking polynomial.
  \item The set of offsets that could have been used for the factor $p_j$ but was not used, which is denoted by $\mathsf{unused\_offset}$ masking polynomial.
\end{enumerate*}
Now, the greediness of the offsets can be established by a zerocheck on the multiplication on these three polynomials which means: It's impossible for a factor $p_j$ to occur in an allowed offset which is smaller than the recorded offset. This is checked in \cref{mfmc:greedy-verdict}.





\begin{DefinePIOP}{Multi-Factor Multi-Character (MFMC) Pattern Matching}{multi-factor-multi-char-pattern-matching}[MFMC Pattern Matching]
  \emergencystretch=3em\relax
  \begin{enumerate}[nosep]
        \item (\emph{Activator validity check})\label[step]{mfmc:validity}
    \begin{enumerate}[nosep]
      \item $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:boolean-check} on $\mathsf{a_c^{new}}$ and on $\mathsf{a_h^{new}}$.
      \item $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:act-cons} on $(\mathsf{a_c^{new}}, \mathsf{a_h^{new}})$.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item (\emph{Filtering by length})\label[step]{mfmc:filter} $\PIOPProver$ computes $\mathsf{a_h^{old'}}$ and $\mathsf{a_c^{old'}}$, the result of filtering out strings shorter than $k:=\sum_{p\in\mathcal{P}}|p|$ (the least length a string can have while containing all factors without overlap), then $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:len-filter} $(\mathsf{a_h^{old}}, \mathsf{a_c^{old}}, \mathsf{a_h^{old'}}, \mathsf{a_c^{old'}})$ with threshold $k$.
    \item (\emph{Containment in the input})\label[step]{mfmc:containment} $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:zerocheck} on $\mathsf{a_h^{new}}\cdot(1-\mathsf{a_h^{old'}})$.
    \item (\emph{Checking patterns separately})\label[step]{mfmc:separate} For each \emph{distinct} $p\in\mathcal{P}$:
    \begin{enumerate}[nosep]
      \item $\PIOPProver$ sends $\mathsf{a^{new}_{h,p}}$ and $\mathsf{a^{new}_{c,p}}$ to $\PIOPVerifier$, and the parties invoke \cref{piop:single-factor-multi-char-pattern-matching} over $p$. This run forms the window-fingerprint difference $\mathsf{diff}_p$ with an attention mask $\mathsf{att\_mask}_{p}$.
      \item $\PIOPProver$ sends the \emph{full occurrence column} $\mathsf{occurs}_p$, which is $1$ at the start of \emph{every} occurrence of $p$. $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ then perform the following steps to establish the validity of $\mathsf{occurs}_p$:
     \begin{enumerate}[nosep]
      \item a \cref{piop:boolean-check} on $\mathsf{occurs}_p$;
      \item a \cref{piop:zerocheck} on $\mathsf{occurs}_p\cdot(1-\mathsf{att\_mask}_{p})$;
      \item a \cref{piop:zerocheck} on $\mathsf{occurs}_p\cdot\mathsf{diff}_p$;
      \item a \cref{piop:nozero-check} on the column with data $\mathsf{diff}_p$ and activator $\mathsf{att\_mask}_{p}\cdot(1-\mathsf{occurs}_p)$.
     \end{enumerate}
    \end{enumerate}
    \item (\emph{Containment in the per-factor match intersection})\label[step]{mfmc:cap-containment} $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ define the per-factor match intersection $\mathsf{a^{\cap}_h}:=\prod_{p\in\mathcal{P}}\mathsf{a^{new}_{h,p}}$ and invoke a \cref{piop:zerocheck} on $\mathsf{a^{new}_{h}}\cdot\bigl(1-\mathsf{a^{\cap}_h}\bigr)$.
    \item \label[step]{mfmc:false-pos} (\emph{False-positive check for factor ordering}) $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ proceed as follows:
    \begin{enumerate}[nosep]
      \item \label[step]{mfmc:false-pos-word-level-occ-ind} \emph{(word-level offset)} For each $j=1,\dots,t$, $\PIOPProver$ computes a word-level column $\mathsf{offset}_{p_j}$ of size $N$ giving the start at which $p_j$ is greedily placed in each word. With frontier $f:=\mathsf{offset}_{p_{j-1}}[i]+|p_{j-1}|$ left by the previous factor ($f:=0$ when $j=1$),
        \[
          \mathsf{offset}_{p_j}[i]:=
          \begin{cases}
            0, & p_j\text{ does not occur in }w_i,\\
            \min\{\,s\ge f : p_j\text{ occurs at }s\,\}, & p_j\text{ occurs at some }s\ge f,\\
            f-|p_j|, & \text{otherwise (}p_j\text{ is stuck).}
          \end{cases}
        \]
        $\PIOPProver$ sends $\mathsf{offset}_{p_j}$ to $\PIOPVerifier$.

   \item \emph{(character-level offset marker)} For each $j=1,\dots,t$, $\PIOPProver$ sends a character-level boolean column $\mathsf{offset\_marker}_{p_j}$ (of size $M$) marking, in each matched word where $p_j$ is placed in order, the character at the placed start: for a character $c$ in word $i:=\mathsf{src}[c]$,
        \[
          \mathsf{offset\_marker}_{p_j}[c]:=
          \begin{cases}
            1, & \mathsf{a^{\cap}_h}[i]=1,\ \mathsf{is\_ordered}_{p_j}[i]=1,\ \text{and}\ \mathsf{o}[c]=\mathsf{offset}_{p_j}[i],\\
            0, & \text{otherwise.}
          \end{cases}
        \]
        $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ then invoke the following to establish its validity:
      \begin{enumerate}[nosep]
        \item a \cref{piop:boolean-check} on $\mathsf{offset\_marker}_{p_j}$;
        \item a \cref{piop:zerocheck} on $\mathsf{offset\_marker}_{p_j}(1-\mathsf{occurs}_{p_j})$;
        \item a \cref{piop:zerocheck} on $\mathsf{offset\_marker}_{p_j}(1-\mathsf{a^{\cap}_c})$;
        \item a \cref{piop:nodup-check} on $\mathsf{src}$ with activator $\mathsf{offset\_marker}_{p_j}$;
        \item two \cref{piop:sumcheck}s for $\sum_{c}\mathsf{offset\_marker}_{p_j}[c]=n$ and $\sum_{i}\mathsf{a^{\cap}_h}[i]\,\mathsf{is\_ordered}_{p_j}[i]=n$, where $n$ is a count sent by $\PIOPProver$.
      \end{enumerate}

      \item \label[step]{mfmc:false-pos-verdict} (\emph{ordering marker}) For each $j=2,\dots,t$, $\PIOPProver$ commits a word-level boolean column $\mathsf{is\_ordered}_{p_j}$ (of size $N$) defined by
        \[
          \mathsf{is\_ordered}_{p_j}[i]\ := \begin{cases}
             1 & \mathsf{offset}_{p_j}[i]\ \ge\ \mathsf{offset}_{p_{j-1}}[i]+|p_{j-1}|
                \,\text{and}\ \mathsf{a^{\cap}_h}[i]=1,\\
             0 & \text{otherwise,}
          \end{cases}
        \]
        with the convention $\mathsf{is\_ordered}_{p_1}:=\mathbf{1}$. $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ then invoke
      \begin{itemize}[nosep]
        \item a \cref{piop:boolean-check} on $\mathsf{is\_ordered}_{p_j}$;
        \item a $\mathsf{NonNeg}$ \cref{piop:sign-check} on $\mathsf{offset}_{p_j}-\mathsf{offset}_{p_{j-1}}-|p_{j-1}|$ with activator $\mathsf{a^{\cap}_h}\,\mathsf{is\_ordered}_{p_j}$;
      \end{itemize}
   
      \item \emph{(consistency between the offset and offset marker)} $\PIOPVerifier$ then samples $\{r_j\}_{j=1}^{t}\samples\F$ and sends them to $\PIOPProver$, then they both run \cref{piop:mset} on $\bigcup_{j=1}^{t}\{\,\mathsf{ind}[i]+r_j\,\mathsf{offset}_{p_j}[i]\ :\ \mathsf{a^{\cap}_h}[i]=1\ \text{and}\ \mathsf{is\_ordered}_{p_j}[i]=1\,\}$ and $\bigcup_{j=1}^{t}\{\,\mathsf{src}[c]+r_j\,\mathsf{o}[c]\ :\ \mathsf{offset\_marker}_{p_j}[c]=1\,\}$.
        \item \emph{(verdict)} For each $j=2,\dots,t$, the parties invoke a \cref{piop:zerocheck} on $\mathsf{a^{new}_h}\,(1-\mathsf{is\_ordered}_{p_j})$.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item \label[step]{mfmc:false-neg}(\emph{False-negative check for factor ordering}) $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ denote by $\mathsf{d_h}:=\mathsf{a^{\cap}_h}-\mathsf{a^{new}_h}$ the \emph{discarded candidates}. 
    \begin{enumerate}[nosep]
      \item (\emph{broadcasting}) $\PIOPProver$ sends the columns $\mathsf{d_h}'$, $\mathsf{offset}'_{p_j}$ for $j=1,\dots,t$ (with $\mathsf{offset}'_{p_0}:=0$), and $\mathsf{is\_ordered}'_{p_j}$ for $j=2,\dots,t$ (with $\mathsf{is\_ordered}'_{p_1}:=\mathbf{1}$), where $x'$ denotes the broadcasted version of $x$. Then $\PIOPProver$ and $\PIOPVerifier$ invoke \cref{piop:broadcast} on all of the broadcasted polynomials.
      \item \label[step]{mfmc:op-well}(\emph{the offset is greedy}) For each $j=1,\dots,t$ the parties invoke:
      \begin{itemize}[nosep]
        \item a $\mathsf{NonNeg}$ \cref{piop:sign-check} on $\mathsf{o}-\bigl(\mathsf{offset}'_{p_{j-1}}+|p_{j-1}|\bigr)$, giving a boolean column $\mathsf{allowed\_offset}_{p_j}$ marking $\mathsf{o}\ge\mathsf{offset}'_{p_{j-1}}+|p_{j-1}|$, with activator $\mathsf{d_h}'$ (so $\mathsf{allowed\_offset}_{p_j}$ flags the offsets from which $p_j$ may still start---those at or past the end of the previously placed factor);
        \item a $\mathsf{Neg}$ \cref{piop:sign-check} on $\mathsf{o}-\mathsf{offset}'_{p_j}$, giving a boolean column $\mathsf{unused\_offset}_{p_j}$ marking $\mathsf{o}<\mathsf{offset}'_{p_j}$, with activator $\mathsf{d_h}'$ (so $\mathsf{unused\_offset}_{p_j}$ flags the offsets strictly before the placement claimed for $p_j$; together, $\mathsf{allowed\_offset}_{p_j}\cdot\mathsf{unused\_offset}_{p_j}$ picks out exactly the window $\mathsf{offset}'_{p_{j-1}}+|p_{j-1}|\le\mathsf{o}<\mathsf{offset}'_{p_j}$);

        \item \cref{piop:zerocheck} on $\mathsf{occurs}_{p_j}\cdot\mathsf{allowed\_offset}_{p_j}$, with activator $\mathsf{d_h}'(1-\mathsf{is\_ordered}'_{p_j})$ (it is impossible for $p_j$ to occur at any offset $\mathsf{o}$ with $\mathsf{o}\ge\mathsf{offset}'_{p_{j-1}}+|p_{j-1}|$, so the scan genuinely has \emph{no} occurrence past the previous factor and is stuck).
        \item \label[step]{mfmc:greedy-verdict} \emph{(verdict)} \cref{piop:zerocheck} on $\mathsf{occurs}_{p_j}\cdot\mathsf{allowed\_offset}_{p_j}\cdot\mathsf{unused\_offset}_{p_j}$, with activator $\mathsf{d_h}'\,\mathsf{is\_ordered}'_{p_j}$ (it is impossible for $p_j$ to occur at an offset $\mathsf{o}$ with $\mathsf{offset}'_{p_{j-1}}+|p_{j-1}|\le\mathsf{o}<\mathsf{offset}'_{p_j}$, so $\mathsf{offset}'_{p_j}$ is the \emph{earliest} occurrence past the previous factor);
      \end{itemize}
      \item (\emph{verdict}) The parties invoke \cref{piop:zerocheck} on $\mathsf{d_h}\cdot\prod_{j=1}^{t}\mathsf{is\_ordered}_{p_j}$.
    \end{enumerate}
  \end{enumerate}
\end{DefinePIOP}
